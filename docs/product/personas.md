# User Personas

## Persona 1: "Lisa" — The Volunteer Scorekeeper

**Role:** Team parent, volunteers to keep score
**Age:** 38
**Tech comfort:** Uses iPhone daily, comfortable with apps but not technical
**Baseball knowledge:** Moderate — learned the basics when her son started playing two years ago

### Context
Lisa signed up to be team parent and got roped into scorekeeping because nobody else volunteered. She's done it for a season and a half now. She sits behind the backstop with the official scorebook, a pen, and a laminated pitch count chart the league handed out at the managers' meeting.

### Pain points
- She's tracking pitch counts on a tally in the margin of the scorebook. When a pitcher gets pulled mid-batter, she has to remember how many pitches were to that batter vs. total. She's gotten it wrong twice.
- The rest-day chart confuses her. She knows 20 is a threshold, but when a kid throws 23 (because they finished the batter after crossing 20), she's not sure if rest days are based on 20 or 23. She asked the coach and got a different answer than what the player agent told her.
- After the game she has to text a summary to three people. She usually does it from the parking lot, typing numbers from the scorebook into her phone. She's sent the wrong pitcher's count to the wrong team column at least once.

### What she needs
- Something that counts for her and tells her when a threshold is crossed
- The app to handle the "finish the batter" math so she doesn't have to
- A way to send the game summary without re-typing everything

### Quote
"I just want something that tells me when to tell the umpire the pitcher needs to come out."

## Persona 2: "Mike" — The Head Coach

**Role:** Team manager, Minors 9/10
**Age:** 44
**Tech comfort:** Uses apps for work (project management, email), not intimidated by new tools
**Baseball knowledge:** High — played through high school, has coached for 6 years

### Context
Mike manages a Minors 9/10 team. He's got 12 kids, about 7 of whom want to pitch. He plans his pitching rotation before each game based on who's available (factoring in rest days from the last game). During the game, he's coaching third base and managing substitutions — he can't also be counting pitches.

### Pain points
- He relies on his scorekeeper (Lisa) for pitch counts, but she can't always give him a number quickly when he yells over from the coach's box
- Before each game, he has to check his notes from the last game to figure out who's eligible to pitch. His notes are in a group text thread and he has to scroll back to find them.
- He's had a situation where he put a kid in to pitch who had caught 4 innings earlier in the game. The other coach caught it and it became a whole thing.

### What he needs
- Quick glance at current pitch count from across the field (big number on screen)
- Game history that shows him which pitchers are available before the next game
- Catcher inning tracking that flags eligibility conflicts before they happen

### Quote
"I need to know two things: how many pitches is he at, and can he still pitch on Thursday."

## Persona 3: "David" — The Player Agent

**Role:** League player agent (board volunteer)
**Age:** 51
**Tech comfort:** Comfortable with spreadsheets, not into apps
**Baseball knowledge:** High — been involved with the league for 8 years

### Context
David is the player agent for EDHLL. He receives game summaries from every team after every game. He's responsible for verifying that no pitcher violated rest-day rules. He gets these summaries via text, email, and occasionally a photo of a paper scoresheet. The formats are all different.

### Pain points
- He gets summaries in 5 different formats. Some are texts with just numbers, some are photos he can barely read, some are detailed emails. He has to normalize all of it into his tracking spreadsheet.
- When a coach reports "23 pitches" he doesn't always know if that means the threshold was 20 (0 rest days) or if the kid actually threw 23 on a clean count (1 rest day). The "finish the batter" rule creates ambiguity in the reports he receives.
- He's had to call coaches mid-week to ask "wait, does your pitcher need rest or not?" because the summary was unclear.

### What he needs
- Consistent summary format from every team
- Clear distinction between actual pitch count and the threshold-based rest-day calculation
- Summaries that arrive promptly (within the required 2 hours)

### Quote
"If every scorekeeper used the same app, my job would take half the time."
